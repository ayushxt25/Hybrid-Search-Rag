# Remote Work Policy

Policy code: HR-REM-204

## Remote Work Eligibility

This policy defines the standard remote-work arrangement for eligible full-time employees. Full-time employees may work remotely up to three days per week when their role, performance expectations, and team coverage requirements support remote work. Remote work is a privilege that depends on continued compliance with information security, attendance, and manager-communication expectations.

New employees must complete a 30-day onboarding period before becoming eligible for recurring remote work. During onboarding, employees are expected to attend required orientation sessions, complete role-specific training, and establish team working norms before submitting a remote-work request.

## Collaboration And Location Rules

Tuesday is the mandatory in-office collaboration day for employees covered by this policy. Teams should use Tuesday for planning sessions, design reviews, customer-readiness meetings, and other activities that benefit from in-person coordination.

Employees working outside their registered state for more than 14 consecutive days require HR approval. The approval requirement helps the company review payroll, tax, insurance, and emergency-contact obligations before extended location changes occur.

## Equipment Allowance And Request Workflow

The remote-work equipment allowance is INR 18,000 once every three years. The allowance may be used for approved equipment such as monitors, ergonomic accessories, or connectivity equipment needed to maintain a productive home workspace.

Requests are submitted through the People Portal. Employees should include the requested remote-work schedule, work location, business justification, and any equipment needs. Managers must respond within five business days so employees can plan their weekly schedule reliably.

## Compliance And Review

Employees must remain reachable during agreed working hours and must follow company security rules when working away from the office. Repeated non-compliance can result in suspension of remote-work privileges. Managers and HR may review remote-work arrangements when business needs, performance issues, or policy exceptions arise.
